Cameroon

Executive Summary

The Problem: Access to water in the village of Mungoa-goa.
  • Mungoa-goa is located on a hillside in the northwest province of Cameroon and consists of approximately 1000 people.
  • The upper third of the people of Mungoa-goa must walk at least 10 min downhill (~300 ft vertical elevation change) for access to clean water
  • WB-USA GT has partnered with the people of Mungoa-goa to provide a solar powered well and distribution system to supply an adequate amount of water according to World Health Organization standards for 20 years.

1st Site Assessment Trip

December 2008

The purpose of this trip was to obtain data in order to develop an engineering solution to the problem and to collect information about the community in general.

Tasks Completed:

  • Surveyed water distribution system
  • Tested water quality
  • Conducted a census of the community
  • Met with nearby Non-Government Organizations (NGOs)

2nd Site Assessment Trip

March 2010

The purpose of this trip was to present the community with our ideas for implementation of a new water collection and distribution system, and collect the data necessary to design and implement the solution on a later trip.

Tasks Completed:

  • Surveyed village (GIS/GPS)
  • Hygiene workshops
  • Built Tippy-Taps
  • Presented solution ideas to village elders:
    • Building a reservoir
    • Collecting rainwater from household roofs
    • Solar-powered well and distribution system
